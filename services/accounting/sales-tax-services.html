What is Sales Tax?

Sales tax is an indirect tax that is paid to a regulating/ governing body for the sale of goods and services. Sales tax is usually charged in percentage (of the worth of the product) at the time of purchase or exchange of products . The amount/ rate of income tax depends upon the ruling government guidelines and individual policies. Sales tax generally terms means the additional money one must buy purchasing goods and services.

Sales Tax in India:

Taxation is one among the important most reasons for the expansion and development of India. In India, we have a dual government structure, which means there are two governments at different levels (union level and state level) and each of them follows a separate but inclined taxation policy.

Central Sales Tax Act, 1956:

The taxation laws, rules/ regulations of the sales tax are contained in Central Sales Tax Act which governs the taxation of sales in India. According to this act, the central government can collect sales tax which has been paid in the state since the sale has been made in the state itself.

State Government Taxes:

To meet financial requirements, the states got to levy a separate tax on sales on the regulations of the ruling government which vary from state to state. The VAT (Value Added Tax) levied by states may be a huge source of income to state governments. And since there's parity in rates, the worth of products varies from state to state.

Sales Tax Exemptions:

On humanitarian grounds or to avoid double taxation, the state governments offer certain tax exemptions:

1- While resale of goods/ products, sales where there's a real state resale certificate, tax is exempted.

2- Sales made to colleges or charities are given tax exemptions.

3- Specific essential and native products are exempted from nuisance tax.

Central Board of Direct Taxes:

The Central Board of direct taxes is a statutory authority that works under the purview of the Central Board Revenue Act and is treated as the apex/ in charge body for the administration of taxes in India. Being a division of the Finance ministry, it works under the Department of Revenue.
